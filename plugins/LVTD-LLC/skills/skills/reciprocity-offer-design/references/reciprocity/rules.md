# Reciprocity Rules

Rules for applying reciprocity without counterfeit evidence or coercive pressure.

## Core Rules

### 1. Make the gift useful on its own

The recipient should benefit even if they never buy, donate, or reciprocate.

### 2. Ask after value, not inside the gift

Do not make the free resource feel like a hidden invoice.

### 3. Let people decline cleanly

Avoid wording that frames refusal as ingratitude.

### 4. Keep concessions transparent

If offering a discount, trade, or downgrade, explain the real basis rather than staging a retreat.

## Guidelines

- Good reciprocity creates goodwill; bad reciprocity creates debt anxiety.
- A free consultation should diagnose honestly, not create pressure to hire the diagnosing party.
- For high-stakes decisions, encourage comparison shopping after the free value is delivered.

## Ethical Boundaries

- Use only honest evidence. Do not invent popularity, scarcity, credentials, endorsements, or social connection.
- Separate helping a good decision from pushing a shortcut response. If the cue is counterfeit, treat it as a red flag.
- When rewriting, preserve user agency: add context, alternatives, and enough time to decide when stakes are meaningful.

## Exceptions

- **Parody, critique, or analysis**: Describe manipulative tactics when the purpose is detection, education, or safer rewriting.
- **Real constraints**: Scarcity, authority, popularity, or reciprocity cues are acceptable when accurate, scoped, and relevant.
- **Tiny low-stakes choices**: Lighter disclosure may be fine, but never fabricate evidence.
