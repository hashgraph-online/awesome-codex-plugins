# Roll Out Experimentation Workflow

Introduce experimentation as a durable product and engineering habit.

## When to Use

- A team or organization is new to A/B testing.
- An A/B testing platform exists but adoption is low.
- Product decisions are made by opinion without measurement.
- Leaders want to grow experiment demand.

## Prerequisites

- At least one product surface that can be measured.
- A team willing to run an early test.
- Basic data or platform path for valid experiments.

**Reference**: `references/core/rules.md`

## Workflow Steps

### Step 1: Diagnose Resistance

**Goal**: Understand what adoption must overcome.

- [ ] Interview product, engineering, data, and leadership stakeholders.
- [ ] List concerns by group.
- [ ] Separate cultural resistance from tool or data blockers.

### Step 2: Choose The First Slice

**Goal**: Prove value with a credible, supported use case.

- [ ] Pick one team and product surface.
- [ ] Choose a decision stakeholders care about.
- [ ] Confirm available metrics and support.

### Step 3: Run With High Support

**Goal**: Make the first experience smooth and useful.

- [ ] Create the brief with the team.
- [ ] Pair on launch and data checks.
- [ ] Prepare the readout together.
- [ ] Capture process friction.

### Step 4: Share Learning

**Goal**: Turn one test into organizational proof.

- [ ] Share the decision and learning.
- [ ] Include neutral and negative results honestly.
- [ ] Add reusable templates or examples.

### Step 5: Expand Deliberately

**Goal**: Grow demand without overwhelming support.

- [ ] Add office hours or review cadence.
- [ ] Track experiments launched and readouts completed.
- [ ] Add governance when volume or risk requires it.

## Exit Criteria

The rollout plan is working when teams request experiments because past tests
helped them make better decisions, not because a mandate requires it.
