# Experimentation Culture Rollout Rules

Rules for adoption planning and resistance handling.

## Core Rules

### 1. Diagnose The Specific Resistance

Do not assume people resist because they dislike data. Ask what they are
protecting: speed, autonomy, revenue, roadmap certainty, user trust, or team
capacity.

### 2. Start With A Real Decision

The first tests should answer questions stakeholders already care about. Toy
experiments rarely build trust.

### 3. Keep Early Process Lightweight

Early adoption fails when the process is heavier than the perceived value.
Start with templates and coaching before adding governance.

### 4. Reframe Losing Tests As Learning

Neutral or negative results can prevent bad launches, reveal segment behavior,
or clarify metric tradeoffs.

### 5. Share Readouts Broadly

Internal stories create demand. Share what was tested, what was learned, what
decision followed, and what will be tested next.

### 6. Add Governance After Usage Grows

Approval rules, naming conventions, and self-service workflows matter, but they
should grow with experiment volume and risk.

## Quick Reference

| Resistance | Practical Response |
|------------|--------------------|
| "Testing slows us down" | Start with one high-value test and reduce ceremony |
| "We know what users want" | Frame test as risk reduction, not a challenge |
| "Bad results hurt the roadmap" | Separate learning from blame |
| "Metrics are not trustworthy" | Start by validating data and definitions |
| "Platform is hard to use" | Provide templates, office hours, and examples |
