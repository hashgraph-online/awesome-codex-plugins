# Ethical Persuasion Examples

Examples are paraphrased applications of the principle, not book excerpts.

## Bad and Better Examples

### Limited beta

Bad: 'Last chance forever.' Better: 'We are opening 50 beta seats because support capacity is limited; applications close Friday.'

### Donation ask

Bad: 'Everyone is giving, don't be the one who refuses.' Better: 'Your donation funds a specific outcome; here is the matching pool and deadline if you want to participate.'

### Onboarding prompt

Bad: 'You promised yourself growth, upgrade now.' Better: 'Set a goal now; after you see your first result, decide whether the paid plan is useful.'

## Rewrite Pattern

1. Identify the active cue.
2. Remove unsupported claims or coercive framing.
3. Add the real evidence, constraint, or user benefit.
4. Make the next step clear but optional.

## Mini Review Example

**Input**: A message uses pressure but does not show evidence.

**Output**:
- Principle: Ethical Persuasion
- Risk: unsupported cue may create a shortcut response.
- Rewrite: state the real reason, proof, or constraint; remove fake pressure.
