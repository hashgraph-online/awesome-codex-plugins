# Choose Seed Channel Workflow

Use this workflow to choose the first seed marketing channel for a useful nonfiction book.

## When To Use

- The author has a mostly working book and needs first readers.
- Multiple launch options are competing for attention.
- The author has limited time, budget, or emotional energy.

## Prerequisites

- Clear book promise.
- Target reader profile.
- Basic evidence that the book creates value.

**Reference**: `references/core/rules.md`

---

## Workflow Steps

### Step 1: Confirm Seed Readiness

**Goal**: Avoid amplifying a weak book.

- [ ] State the promise.
- [ ] State the target reader.
- [ ] Name evidence that readers receive value.
- [ ] Name the recommendation story.

---

### Step 2: Inventory Author Assets

**Goal**: Choose from strengths and constraints.

- [ ] Existing audience or newsletter.
- [ ] Speaking, teaching, or podcast comfort.
- [ ] Consulting, client, or event relationships.
- [ ] Budget for ads or giveaways.
- [ ] Time available per week.
- [ ] Desire for a long-term platform.

---

### Step 3: Score Channels

**Goal**: Compare options pragmatically.

Use this table:

```text
Channel | Reader fit | Author fit | Speed | Cost | Long-term value | Risk | Score
```

Score:

- Digital book tour.
- Amazon PPC.
- Event giveaways or bulk sales.
- Writing in public.

---

### Step 4: Pick One Primary Channel

**Goal**: Avoid scattered effort.

- [ ] Choose the easiest viable primary channel.
- [ ] Choose at most one supporting channel.
- [ ] Define what success looks like after 2-6 weeks.
- [ ] Define the first ten actions.

---

### Step 5: Add Email Capture

**Goal**: Convert attention into reachable audience.

- [ ] Choose signup, pre-order, or email-me flow.
- [ ] Make the call to action specific.
- [ ] Segment beta readers from finished-book buyers when relevant.

## Exit Criteria

Task is complete when:

- [ ] One primary channel is selected.
- [ ] The author has a short action plan.
- [ ] There is a direct interest-capture path.
