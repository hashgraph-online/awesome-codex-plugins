# Plan Writing In Public Workflow

Use this workflow to turn manuscript work into consistent public content.

## When To Use

- The author wants to build an audience while writing.
- The book is tied to a long-term business, career, or body of work.
- The author has research, drafts, beta insights, or process material to share.

## Prerequisites

- Target reader profile.
- At least one place to publish.
- A capture path for interested readers.

**Reference**: `references/core/examples.md`

---

## Workflow Steps

### Step 1: Choose Content Sources

**Goal**: Use work already being done.

- [ ] Draft excerpts.
- [ ] Research notes.
- [ ] Deleted sections.
- [ ] Reader conversations or beta lessons.
- [ ] Process or progress updates.

---

### Step 2: Build The Sharing List

**Goal**: Know where each piece belongs.

- [ ] List owned channels.
- [ ] List relevant communities.
- [ ] List newsletters or blogs that accept contributions.
- [ ] List podcasts or online events.
- [ ] Record rules and cultural norms.

---

### Step 3: Create A Weekly Cadence

**Goal**: Make publishing consistent.

- [ ] Choose the minimum sustainable frequency.
- [ ] Batch source material.
- [ ] Repurpose each substantial source into smaller pieces.
- [ ] Schedule posts ahead when possible.

---

### Step 4: Add A Capture Path

**Goal**: Convert attention into audience.

- [ ] Link to signup, pre-order, beta-reader form, or email address.
- [ ] Explain what people will receive.
- [ ] Keep the call to action respectful and relevant.

---

### Step 5: Review What Works

**Goal**: Learn from target-reader response.

- [ ] Track replies, signups, and useful conversations.
- [ ] Note which topics create reader pull.
- [ ] Feed reader language back into the book and marketing.

## Exit Criteria

Task is complete when:

- [ ] The author has a repeatable content cadence.
- [ ] The content comes from existing book work.
- [ ] Interested readers have a direct next step.
