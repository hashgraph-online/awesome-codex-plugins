# Book Seed Marketing Knowledge

Core concepts for seeding a useful nonfiction book.

## Overview

Seed marketing creates the initial audience that can receive value and begin recommending the book. It works best when the book has already been designed and tested for recommendability.

## Key Concepts

### Seed Audience

**Definition**: The first target readers who receive enough value to become potential recommenders.

The goal is not raw exposure. The goal is getting the book to readers close enough to the ideal profile that they can benefit and talk about it.

### Temporary Marketing

**Definition**: A focused push to create initial traction, after which organic growth can carry more of the load.

Useful nonfiction should not require endless manual promotion. If it does, the book may not be working or may not have reached the right readers.

### Digital Book Tour

**Definition**: Remote appearances on podcasts, webinars, newsletters, online events, or communities where the author teaches useful material.

This is scalable because the author can appear from anywhere and reuse the book's core ideas.

### Amazon PPC

**Definition**: Paid ads inside Amazon that help interested book shoppers discover the book.

Ads are often easy to start but limited by click volume, cover clarity, pricing, and per-copy economics.

### Event Giveaway Or Bulk Sale

**Definition**: Getting a relevant event, company, school, or community to put the book into many ideal readers' hands at once.

This can seed quickly when the event audience closely matches the reader profile.

### Writing In Public

**Definition**: Sharing useful parts of the book process, research, drafts, lessons, and progress while the book is being created.

This can build a compounding audience, but it requires consistency and emotional tolerance for public work.

### Email Capture

**Definition**: Turning interest from public posts, events, or excerpts into direct contact permission.

Email is usually more valuable than social following because it is direct, portable, and launchable.

## Terminology

| Term | Definition |
|------|------------|

| Channel fit | Match between reader behavior, author strengths, and marketing channel. |
| Lead magnet | Useful free resource exchanged for email permission. |
| Writing in public | Publishing parts of the work/process while building the book. |
| Momentum launch | Sharing real progress, milestones, and reader signals as launch content. |

## How It Relates To

- **Book TOC Lab**: Strong promise and reader profile make channel selection possible.
- **Beta Reader Feedback**: Beta readers can become testimonials, advocates, or early launch supporters.
- **Book Sales Optimization**: Seed marketing starts demand; optimization improves conversion after demand exists.

## Common Misconceptions

- **Myth**: Launch is a single day.
  **Reality**: Seed marketing often works over weeks or months.

- **Myth**: Every author needs a big platform first.
  **Reality**: A useful book can be seeded through targeted channels even without a large audience.

- **Myth**: Content marketing requires inventing new material.
  **Reality**: The manuscript, research, deleted sections, and beta insights are raw material.

- **Myth**: Any giveaway counts.
  **Reality**: A giveaway only helps if recipients are close to the ideal reader profile.
