# Book Seed Marketing Rules

Use these rules when planning early audience creation for a useful nonfiction book.

## Core Rules

### 1. Do Not Seed A Broken Book

Seed marketing amplifies the book's current quality.

- If beta readers do not receive value, revise first.
- If the promise is vague, sharpen it first.
- If the reader profile is broad, narrow it first.

### 2. Optimize For Target Reader Fit

A small number of ideal readers beats a large number of irrelevant readers.

- Choose channels where target readers already gather.
- Avoid vanity exposure that cannot create readers or recommenders.
- Evaluate events by reader fit, not size alone.

### 3. Choose The Easiest Viable Channel

Channel fit depends on the author.

- Public speaker or teacher: digital tour and events may be easiest.
- Consultant with client access: bulk sales and giveaways may be easiest.
- Patient self-publisher with clear cover: Amazon PPC may be easiest.
- Long-term domain builder: writing in public may be highest leverage.

### 4. Repurpose Existing Work

Use the book as its own marketing.

- Turn excerpts into posts.
- Turn research into articles or videos.
- Turn deleted sections into standalone content.
- Turn beta insights into progress updates.

### 5. Build A Sharing List Before Posting

Do not improvise distribution every time.

- List communities, newsletters, podcasts, events, and social spaces.
- Record submission rules and cultural norms.
- Match each piece of content to the right place.

### 6. Create Accountability

Writing in public fails when posting depends on mood.

- Use a weekly schedule.
- Batch content when possible.
- Reuse one source item across several formats.
- Keep the cadence sustainable.

### 7. Capture Direct Interest

Do not leave all interest on rented channels.

- Add an email signup, pre-order, or simple email-me flow.
- Offer beta-reader and finished-book options when the book is still in progress.
- Make the signup valuable and honest.

## Guidelines

- One strong seed channel is enough to start.
- PPC is a complement when economics and cover clarity work.
- Giveaways should be frictionless for recipients.
- Digital appearances should teach value, not merely announce a book.
- If the book supports a future business, platform building may be worth the extra time.

## Exceptions

- **Already large platform**: The author may seed through owned audience first, but should still prioritize target-reader fit.
- **Highly visual/lifestyle topics**: Image or video-heavy channels may outperform typical nonfiction channels.
- **Traditional publisher constraints**: Coordinate timing, pricing, and review requests with the publisher.

## Quick Reference

| Rule | Summary |
|------|---------|
| Fix book first | Marketing cannot rescue low value. |
| Fit beats volume | Ideal readers matter most. |
| Easiest viable | Choose channel by assets and constraints. |
| Repurpose work | The manuscript is source material. |
| Sharing list | Know where content belongs. |
| Accountability | Schedule beats mood. |
| Capture interest | Turn attention into reachable audience. |
