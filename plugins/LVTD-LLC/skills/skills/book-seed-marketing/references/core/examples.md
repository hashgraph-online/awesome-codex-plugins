# Book Seed Marketing Examples

Synthetic examples for channel selection and seed plans.

## Channel Fit Examples

### Consultant With Corporate Clients

**Likely best channel**: Event giveaways and bulk sales.

**Why**:
- Existing relationships can create large batches of ideal readers.
- Books can support consulting or training.
- A custom foreword, workshop bundle, or team distribution may increase value.

**Watch out**:
- Recipients must be ideal readers.
- Event organizers take reputational risk when distributing a book.

### Self-Published Author With Clear Amazon Page

**Likely best channel**: Amazon PPC plus light writing in public.

**Why**:
- Searchers are already in book-buying mode.
- Ads can slowly build seed readers.
- Higher self-publishing royalties can support paid clicks.

**Watch out**:
- Ads depend on cover/title clarity.
- Click volume may not scale far.
- Start with small budgets.

### Author Building A Long-Term Domain Business

**Likely best channel**: Writing in public and email capture.

**Why**:
- Audience compounds beyond one book.
- Research, drafts, and process can become ongoing content.
- The book can become a relationship asset.

**Watch out**:
- Requires consistency.
- Needs a capture mechanism, not just social posts.

## Writing In Public Content Sources

| Source | Possible Content |
|--------|------------------|
| Draft section | Excerpt, annotated screenshot, short lesson, thread, newsletter. |
| Deleted section | Behind-the-scenes lesson or bonus article. |
| Research note | Link commentary, interview clip, quote analysis, case study. |
| Beta feedback | Progress update, lesson learned, reader question. |
| Milestone | Pre-order rank, review, first royalty, launch retrospective. |

## Simple Email Capture

### Full Setup

```text
Landing page -> useful lead magnet -> email signup -> beta-reader or launch list segment
```

**Best when**:
- The author has a site or newsletter.
- The lead magnet is genuinely useful.
- There will be future updates.

### Low-Tech Setup

```text
Public post -> "email me if you want the book when it launches" -> dedicated inbox -> manual replies
```

**Best when**:
- The author wants speed.
- The audience is small.
- Personal replies are a feature, not a burden.

## Two-Week Starter Plan

```text
Week 1
- List 25 places where target readers gather.
- Pull 10 useful excerpts or research notes from the manuscript.
- Create a simple email capture path.
- Publish 2 pieces of content.

Week 2
- Pitch 5 podcasts, newsletters, or communities.
- Publish 2 more pieces of content.
- Invite interested readers to beta read or join the launch list.
- Review which channel created target-reader replies.
```
