# Book Seed Marketing Checklist

Use this checklist before planning a seed push.

## Seed Readiness

- [ ] Book promise is clear.
- [ ] Target reader is specific.
- [ ] Beta or early readers have received value.
- [ ] Reader experience is strong enough to create recommendations.
- [ ] The book has a clear recommendation story.

## Channel Selection

- [ ] Digital tour is considered if the author can teach well in public.
- [ ] Amazon PPC is considered if the cover, title, subtitle, pricing, and royalties support it.
- [ ] Event giveaways are considered if relevant events or organizations gather ideal readers.
- [ ] Writing in public is considered if the author wants long-term audience value.
- [ ] The easiest viable path is chosen instead of the most impressive one.

## Distribution Prep

- [ ] List of communities, podcasts, newsletters, events, and partner audiences exists.
- [ ] Submission rules and culture are recorded.
- [ ] Content ideas come from manuscript, research, process, and deleted material.
- [ ] Content schedule is realistic.
- [ ] Email capture or pre-order path exists.

## Metrics

- [ ] Target-reader replies or conversations.
- [ ] Email signups or pre-orders.
- [ ] Beta-reader volunteers.
- [ ] Books distributed to ideal readers.
- [ ] Paid ad sales and cost per sale when using PPC.
- [ ] Invitations to speak, write, or appear.

## Red Flags

- The author wants traffic before the book has proven value.
- Channel choice is based on trendiness rather than reader fit.
- Giveaway audience is broad or irrelevant.
- Content has no capture path.
- PPC starts with a high budget before proving profitability.

## Quick Reference

| Aspect | Ideal | Acceptable | Red Flag |
|--------|-------|------------|----------|
| Book | Proven useful | Promising with beta signals | Untested |
| Audience | Ideal reader concentration | Mixed but reachable | Broad exposure |
| Channel | Fits author assets | Learnable | Emotionally impossible |
| Capture | Direct email/pre-order | Simple inbox flow | No follow-up path |
