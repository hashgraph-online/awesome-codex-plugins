# Beta Reader Feedback Examples

Synthetic examples for setting up and interpreting beta feedback.

## Reader Instructions

### Early Structural Round

```text
Thanks for reading this rough draft. Please do not worry about typos unless they block understanding. The most useful comments are:

- Where you get confused.
- Where you disagree or feel skeptical.
- Where you expected an example, caveat, or missing step.
- Where you get bored or feel tempted to stop.
- Where something is genuinely useful and worth expanding.
```

### Later Polish Round

```text
This draft has already been through structural beta reading. Please focus on:

- Factual mistakes.
- Overclaims or missing caveats.
- Awkward phrasing.
- Typos.
- Any final examples or explanations that still feel unclear.
```

## Feedback Classification

### Raw Comment

```text
I am not sure this applies to nonprofit teams. The example assumes a sales-driven company.
```

**Signal**: Reader-profile mismatch, missing caveat, or overgeneralization.

**Possible revisions**:
- Add a nonprofit example.
- State that the section is for sales-led companies.
- Move nonprofit teams out of scope.
- Change the claim from universal to conditional.

## Comment Drop-Off

### Observation

```text
Reader A left 18 comments in Chapter 1, 9 in Chapter 2, and none after the first page of Chapter 3.
Reader B stopped commenting midway through Chapter 3.
Reader C wrote "interesting" at the start of Chapter 3 and then disappeared.
```

**Likely signal**: Chapter 3 or the end of Chapter 2 has low value-per-page, missing setup, or unresolved confusion.

**Next action**:
- Audit the surrounding sections.
- Cut or compress the slowest material.
- Move a stronger payoff earlier.
- Ask follow-up questions if the reader is willing.

## Positive Feedback

### Raw Comment

```text
This example finally made the concept click. I used it in a meeting this morning.
```

**Signal**: Evidence of value and real-world transfer.

**Possible revisions**:
- Preserve the example.
- Add more examples like it.
- Move it earlier if it unlocks later material.
- Use this kind of reader language in marketing later.

## Defensive Reaction

### Author Thought

```text
They misunderstood this because they did not read carefully.
```

**Reframe**:

```text
If this reader misunderstood it, others may too. What can the manuscript do differently so the right reader cannot miss the point?
```

**Possible revisions**:
- Simplify the language.
- Add a transition.
- Define the term earlier.
- Remove idiom or insider phrasing.
