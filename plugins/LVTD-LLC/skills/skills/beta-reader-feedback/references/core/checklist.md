# Beta Reader Feedback Checklist

Use this checklist before, during, and after a beta reading round.

## Before Inviting Readers

- [ ] Target reader profile is clear.
- [ ] Draft is coherent enough to follow.
- [ ] Feedback tool supports live comments.
- [ ] Readers can comment or suggest, not accidentally rewrite the manuscript.
- [ ] Feedback instructions match the manuscript stage.
- [ ] Influential readers are saved until the draft is strong enough.

## Recruitment

- [ ] Desired number of deeply engaged readers is defined.
- [ ] Invitation list is larger than the desired number.
- [ ] Readers resemble the target reader.
- [ ] Readers know the expected time commitment.
- [ ] The author is ready for negative feedback.

## During The Round

- [ ] Do not pressure readers to finish.
- [ ] Watch where comments stop.
- [ ] Note excitement and value signals.
- [ ] Note confusion, skepticism, and missing information.
- [ ] Avoid debating comments inside the document.

## Synthesis

- [ ] Comments are grouped by signal type.
- [ ] Repeated problems are prioritized above one-off preferences.
- [ ] Abandonment points are mapped to sections.
- [ ] Positive value signals are preserved and amplified.
- [ ] Expert or factual issues are verified.
- [ ] Revision priorities are ordered before editing begins.

## Finish Signals

- [ ] It is easy to recruit the right readers.
- [ ] Most target readers receive value and reach the end.
- [ ] At least some readers recommend or want to share the manuscript.
- [ ] Remaining issues are polish, accuracy, layout, or final examples.

## Red Flags

- Readers only leave typos in an early round.
- The author explains every negative comment away.
- Multiple readers disappear at the same section.
- Friends finish out of obligation but show little excitement.
- The manuscript enters pre-sale before structure and value are working.
