# LinkedIn Comment Writing Rules

## Comment Quality Rules

- Read the original post before drafting. Do not comment from the headline.
- Add information the original post did not already contain.
- Prefer concrete examples, useful distinctions, and good questions.
- Keep the comment short enough to be read in-thread.
- Use the author's vocabulary where appropriate, but do not mimic them.
- Do not make every comment about the user's product or service.

## Discovery Rules

- Comment where the target audience already gathers.
- Use relevant hashtags and topic searches to find posts worth joining.
- Commenting on posts with existing discussion can increase visibility, but only
  join when the comment adds value.
- Do not chase large threads if the user's audience is not present.

## Reply Rules For The User's Own Posts

- Reply to early comments quickly when possible.
- Answer with more than "thanks" when the commenter opened a useful path.
- Ask follow-up questions when they improve the discussion.
- Like or acknowledge comments when no substantive reply is needed.
- Avoid escalating hostile threads unless there is a clear professional reason.

## Risk Rules

- Do not write generic comments such as "Great post" unless adding a real point.
- Do not incentivize comments without flagging competition or policy risk.
- Do not recommend comment pods, fake comments, or purchased engagement.
- Do not automate comments.
- Do not use AI-generated comment suggestions unchanged if they sound generic.
