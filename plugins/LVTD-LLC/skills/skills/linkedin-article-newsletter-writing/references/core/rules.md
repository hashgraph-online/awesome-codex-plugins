# LinkedIn Article Newsletter Rules

## Strategy Rules

- State the goal before drafting: expertise proof, teaching, series-building,
  lead-in to a conversation, or profile asset.
- Define the target reader and why they would save or subscribe.
- Keep a newsletter focused on one recognizable topic.
- Choose a cadence the author can sustain.
- Use long-form when the topic deserves depth; do not pad a post into an
  article.

## Drafting Rules

- Do not write promotional articles disguised as education.
- Lead with the reader problem or useful promise.
- Use headings that make the piece scannable.
- Include examples, steps, frameworks, or implications.
- End with a short author bio or relevant next step.
- For "why follow me" articles, state what readers will consistently get.

## Distribution Rules

- Write a short teaser post that summarizes the article or asks a related
  question.
- Reply to comments on the article or teaser.
- Use relevant hashtags when they help discovery.
- Consider featuring durable articles where profile surfaces support it.

## Current-Platform Rules

- Check current LinkedIn help before stating who can create newsletters, what
  Page requirements apply, or how many newsletters are allowed.
- Check current LinkedIn help before advising on Live, events, audio, or other
  changing formats.
- Track newsletter analytics when the user wants improvement over time.
