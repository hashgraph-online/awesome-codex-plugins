# LinkedIn Post Writing Rules

## Content Rules

- Write for a target audience and a topic the author can credibly own.
- Make industry news useful by adding interpretation, consequences, or a next
  action.
- Prefer first-person professional perspective when the post depends on the
  author's experience.
- Connect the author to a topic through examples, photos, events, artifacts, or
  proof, not through generic self-promotion.
- Keep company content useful to readers; do not make the post a press release
  unless the audience has a reason to care.

## Structure Rules

- Make the first 2-3 visible lines earn the click or continued reading.
- Use short paragraphs and visible progression for posts near the character
  limit.
- Use a longer text post, document, or native video when the goal is dwell time.
- Keep videos short and add captions or review auto-captions when relevant.
- Add image alt text when recommending images.
- Avoid fancy Unicode styling for important words because screen readers may
  handle it poorly.

## Hashtag And Mention Rules

- Use a small set of relevant hashtags. Do not stuff tags for reach.
- Use hashtags that match the author's topic territory and likely audience
  discovery behavior.
- Mention people or Pages only when there is a real relationship to the post.
- Avoid tagging people just to force a notification.
- If the post references an author, speaker, source, company, or event, a
  mention can be useful when it is contextually fair.

## Link Rules

- If a link is essential, do not hide it in a way that frustrates readers.
- Avoid editing a post after publication just to add the link unless there is a
  clear reason.
- Use a post to summarize why the link matters; do not drop a bare URL.

## Conversation Rules

- End with a question only when the author genuinely wants answers.
- Reply to comments promptly, especially early comments.
- Do not ask for likes or comments in a way that feels mechanical or desperate.
- If offering an incentive for comments, flag competition, legal, and community
  guideline risk.

## Safety Rules

- Do not recommend engagement pods, bought likes, bought comments, fake
  followers, or algorithm manipulation.
- Do not recommend scraping, browser extensions, or automated activity that
  violates LinkedIn policy.
- Treat "power engagement" as a lesson in risk: seek expert reaction by writing
  something worth engaging with, not by buying engagement.
