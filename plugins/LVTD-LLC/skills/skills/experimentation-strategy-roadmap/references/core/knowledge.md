# Experimentation Strategy Roadmap Knowledge

Core concepts for prioritizing experimentation platform and process investments.

## Overview

Mature experimentation programs improve through a mix of optimization
strategies, process improvements, and infrastructure enhancements. The right
roadmap depends on company goals, user needs, platform robustness, team adoption,
and the tradeoff between speed, quality, and cost.

## Key Concepts

### Optimization Strategy

**Definition**: A method that changes how teams evaluate product changes, such
as variance reduction, interleaving, overlapping tests, or adaptive testing.

Optimization strategies can improve rate, quality, or cost, but often require
new tools and process guidance.

### Process Improvement

**Definition**: A change to how teams plan, launch, monitor, analyze, or learn
from experiments.

Examples include gold-standard checklists, playbooks, workshops, and decision
frameworks.

### Infrastructure Enhancement

**Definition**: Tooling, data, dashboards, libraries, or platform capability
needed to support experimentation practices.

Infrastructure should make good experimentation easier, not just add complexity.

### Platformization

**Definition**: Turning a proven method or workflow into a reusable platform
capability for many teams.

Platformization is justified when use cases repeat, value is clear, and the
method can be made understandable and maintainable.

### Data-Influenced Decision Making

**Definition**: Using experiment evidence to shape decisions while recognizing
that data informs rather than automatically dictates every choice.

This framing keeps experimentation connected to strategy and stakeholder
judgment.

## Terminology

| Term | Definition |
|------|------------|
| Rate | How quickly teams can run and decide from experiments |
| Quality | Trustworthiness and usefulness of experiment evidence |
| Cost | User, business, engineering, and time cost of experimentation |
| Adoption | Whether teams can understand and use a capability correctly |
| Robustness | Whether platform systems remain reliable as usage and methods grow |

## How It Relates To

- **A/B testing platform strategy**: This skill chooses roadmap priorities; the
  platform skill scopes architecture and build/buy strategy.
- **Experiment verification monitoring**: Quality investments often start with
  verification, canaries, A/A tests, and monitoring.
- **Experimentation throughput strategy**: Rate investments often start with
  capacity visibility and overlap policies.

## Common Misconceptions

- **Myth**: Advanced methods should be platformized as soon as they work once.
  **Reality**: Repeated use cases, adoption, and maintainability matter.
- **Myth**: Speed and quality are separate roadmap tracks.
  **Reality**: Speed gains often require quality safeguards to remain useful.
- **Myth**: More tooling automatically improves experimentation.
  **Reality**: Tools need process, education, and usability to change behavior.
