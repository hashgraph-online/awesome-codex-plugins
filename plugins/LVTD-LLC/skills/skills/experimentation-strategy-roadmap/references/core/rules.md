# Experimentation Strategy Roadmap Rules

Use these rules when prioritizing experimentation investments.

## Core Rules

### 1. Anchor Roadmap Work In Company Goals

Experimentation platform work should support strategic priorities.

- Identify which product or business decisions need better evidence.
- Prioritize surfaces and teams tied to current goals.
- Avoid building capabilities disconnected from roadmap needs.

### 2. Balance Rate, Quality, And Cost

Do not optimize one dimension blindly.

- Rate without quality creates unreliable decisions.
- Quality without usability creates slow adoption.
- Cost reduction without evidence quality creates false economy.

### 3. Pair Advanced Methods With Process And Infrastructure

Advanced techniques require enablement.

- Add templates, education, and decision rules.
- Add data, logging, dashboards, and monitoring.
- Add ownership and support paths.

### 4. Prototype Before Platformizing

One successful use case is not enough.

- Test the method on representative use cases.
- Score experimentation design value, engineering complexity, and product need.
- Platformize only when repeated value justifies ongoing maintenance.

### 5. Optimize For Platform Users

The platform succeeds only if teams can use it correctly.

- Keep workflows simple where possible.
- Provide defaults and templates for common experiment types.
- Avoid exposing unnecessary statistical or engineering complexity.

## Guidelines

Less strict recommendations:

- Use workshops to discover pain points across novice and expert platform users.
- Track platform success metrics such as setup time, invalid test rate, capacity,
  self-service usage, and decision latency.
- Revisit roadmap priorities after major product or company strategy changes.

## Exceptions

- **High-risk strategic pivot**: Prioritize evidence quality even if throughput
  slows temporarily.
- **Rapid exploration period**: Prioritize low-cost learning if decisions are
  reversible and stakes are low.

## Quick Reference

| Rule | Summary |
|------|---------|

| Balance dimensions | Rate, quality, and cost interact |
