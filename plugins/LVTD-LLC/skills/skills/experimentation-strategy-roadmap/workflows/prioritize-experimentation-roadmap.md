# Prioritize Experimentation Roadmap Workflow

Choose the next experimentation platform or process investment.

## When to Use

- The team has many possible experimentation improvements.
- Leadership asks whether to prioritize rate, quality, or cost.
- A team wants to platformize an advanced method.
- Experimentation tooling feels powerful but hard to adopt.

## Prerequisites

- Company or product strategic goals.
- Current experimentation volume, pain points, and quality risks.
- Candidate initiatives.
- Team capacity and adoption constraints.

**Reference**: `references/core/rules.md`

## Workflow Steps

### Step 1: Anchor On Strategy

**Goal**: Keep platform work connected to business and product goals.

- [ ] Name the strategic goals experimentation should support.
- [ ] Identify which teams or product surfaces matter most now.
- [ ] Define whether the main pain is rate, quality, cost, or adoption.

### Step 2: Classify Candidate Work

**Goal**: See what kind of investment each idea requires.

- [ ] Mark each candidate as optimization strategy, process improvement,
  infrastructure enhancement, or a combination.
- [ ] Identify dependencies between tools, process, documentation, and training.
- [ ] Remove candidates with no clear use case.

### Step 3: Score Tradeoffs

**Goal**: Compare work by outcome, not novelty.

- [ ] Score impact on experimentation rate.
- [ ] Score impact on insight quality and trust.
- [ ] Score cost reduction or cost increase.
- [ ] Score usability and adoption effort.
- [ ] Score engineering complexity and robustness risk.

### Step 4: Prototype Before Platformizing

**Goal**: Avoid large investments in unproven methods.

- [ ] Pick 1-3 representative use cases for the advanced method.
- [ ] Run minimal implementation or manual workflow.
- [ ] Decide whether the method merits platform support based on evidence.

### Step 5: Sequence The Roadmap

**Goal**: Make adoption feasible.

- [ ] Put prerequisite infrastructure before broad rollout.
- [ ] Pair new methods with templates, docs, and quality checks.
- [ ] Define platform success metrics and review cadence.

## Quick Checklist

```text
[ ] Strategic goals named
[ ] Current rate/quality/cost/adoption pain identified
[ ] Candidate initiatives classified
[ ] Tradeoffs scored
[ ] Advanced methods prototyped before platformization
[ ] Roadmap includes tooling, process, and education
```

## Exit Criteria

The roadmap is ready when the next investment is justified by strategy,
evidence, adoption needs, and a clear tradeoff against alternatives.
