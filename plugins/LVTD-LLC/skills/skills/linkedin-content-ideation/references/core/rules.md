# LinkedIn Content Ideation Rules

## Territory Rules

- Define what the author wants to be associated with before brainstorming.
- Reject ideas that are interesting but outside the desired topic territory.
- Balance consistency with variety: multiple formats can support one topic.
- Use personal examples to make a broad topic ownable.

## Idea Generation Rules

- Convert every source into a post angle, not only a headline.
- Pair news with interpretation.
- Pair questions with a practical answer or point of view.
- Pair personal stories with a transferable lesson.
- Pair company content with reader benefit.
- Pair visual ideas with the message the visual should make easier to grasp.

## Prioritization Rules

Score ideas by:

- Audience value.
- Author credibility.
- Freshness or timing.
- Fit with the author's topic territory.
- Ease of producing a useful asset.
- Test value: what the author can learn from publishing it.

## Calendar Rules

- Build a backlog before building a calendar.
- Keep the minimum sustainable cadence.
- Do not fill a calendar with weak posts just to be daily.
- Use scheduling tools only after the draft is good and the author can respond
  to comments.

## Safety Rules

- Do not recommend trend-jacking unrelated topics.
- Do not rewrite others' posts without adding original interpretation.
- Do not recommend automated scraping or engagement manipulation.
- Flag platform-current assumptions before naming tools or features.
