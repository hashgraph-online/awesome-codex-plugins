# Validate TOC Workflow

Use this workflow to test a nonfiction book promise and table of contents before drafting.

## When To Use

- A user has a book idea but no draft.
- A draft outline feels broad, vague, or hard to recommend.
- The user wants to test a book before writing full chapters.

## Prerequisites

- A working book promise.
- A target reader profile.
- A rough chapter or section list.

**Reference**: `references/core/rules.md`

---

## Workflow Steps

### Step 1: Convert The Idea Into A Book Brief

**Goal**: Make the reader, promise, and scope explicit.

- [ ] Identify the target reader and their current situation.
- [ ] State the reader problem or goal in the reader's language.
- [ ] Write the book promise in one or two sentences.
- [ ] List what the book will not cover.
- [ ] Define what success looks like after reading.

**Reference**: `references/core/checklist.md`

---

### Step 2: Write The Recommendation Story

**Goal**: Test whether word of mouth has a plausible path.

- [ ] Write the situation where the problem naturally comes up.
- [ ] Identify who would recommend the book.
- [ ] Identify who receives the recommendation.
- [ ] State why this book is the obvious fit.
- [ ] Revise the promise or reader if the story feels weak.

**Reference**: `references/core/knowledge.md`

---

### Step 3: Rewrite The TOC As Takeaways

**Goal**: Turn the TOC into an education design.

- [ ] Replace topic labels with reader outcomes.
- [ ] Add subsections for distinct learning outcomes.
- [ ] Mark the practical output each chapter creates.
- [ ] Remove or move chapters that do not support the promise.
- [ ] Put the first substantial value as early as possible.

**Reference**: `references/core/examples.md`

---

### Step 4: Run Reader Conversation Tests

**Goal**: Validate the plan before prose exists.

- [ ] Ask listening questions about the reader's lived problem.
- [ ] Avoid asking whether they like the book idea.
- [ ] Teach one or two sections from the TOC.
- [ ] Note where the reader gets confused, skeptical, excited, or bored.
- [ ] Revise the TOC before drafting.

---

### Step 5: Decide Whether To Draft

**Goal**: Move only when the foundation is strong enough.

- [ ] The promise is desirable to the target reader.
- [ ] The reader can understand the TOC without a long explanation.
- [ ] The recommendation story is plausible.
- [ ] The author knows which parts need proof, examples, or exercises.

## Exit Criteria

Task is complete when:

- [ ] The book brief is concise and specific.
- [ ] The TOC is takeaway-first.
- [ ] At least one reader-test or simulated reader-test has produced revisions.
- [ ] The next drafting step is clear.
