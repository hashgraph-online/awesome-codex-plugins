# Book TOC Lab Checklist

Use this checklist before drafting a reader-outcome-focused nonfiction book.

## Book Brief

- [ ] Target reader is a recognizable person in a recognizable situation.
- [ ] Promise states a concrete outcome, not a topic.
- [ ] Starting assumptions are explicit.
- [ ] Success evidence is observable.
- [ ] The book says who it is not for.
- [ ] Out-of-scope topics are listed.

## Recommendability

- [ ] The reader problem is urgent or valuable enough to discuss.
- [ ] The recommendation story has a trigger, recommender, and recipient.
- [ ] The book can be described in one or two sentences.
- [ ] The book is positioned to be best for someone, not second-best for everyone.
- [ ] The promise can remain relevant for several years.
- [ ] Time-sensitive tactics are excluded or moved to companion material.

## TOC Quality

- [ ] Chapter titles describe reader takeaways.
- [ ] Subsections expose major learning outcomes.
- [ ] Every chapter directly supports the promise.
- [ ] The first chapter delivers real value quickly.
- [ ] Prerequisites appear before they are needed.
- [ ] No chapter exists only because the author wants to include it.

## Validation

- [ ] Listening conversations have explored reader reality before pitching the idea.
- [ ] Teaching conversations have tested at least one high-value chapter or section.
- [ ] Feedback is tracked as confusion, skepticism, boredom, excitement, or missing content.
- [ ] The TOC has changed based on what readers needed.

## Red Flags

- The book needs a long explanation before anyone understands who it is for.
- The TOC is made of topics instead of outcomes.
- Readers respond politely but do not ask follow-up questions.
- The author cannot find even one potential reader to talk to.
- The author is adding chapters to avoid making scope decisions.

## Quick Reference

| Aspect | Ideal | Acceptable | Red Flag |
|--------|-------|------------|----------|
| Reader | Specific role and moment | Specific role | Everyone |
| Promise | Observable outcome | Clear benefit | Broad topic |
| Scope | Strong exclusions | Some exclusions | Covers everything |
| TOC | Takeaway titles | Mixed titles | Topic labels |
| Validation | Real reader tests | Simulated reader review | No testing |
