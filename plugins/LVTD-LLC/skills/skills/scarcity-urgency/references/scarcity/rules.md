# Scarcity and Urgency Rules

Rules for applying scarcity and urgency without counterfeit evidence or coercive pressure.

## Core Rules

### 1. Prove the constraint

Know whether scarcity comes from inventory, capacity, deadline, eligibility, seasonality, or policy.

### 2. Avoid invented deadlines

Do not create timers or claims that reset, follow the user, or lack operational reason.

### 3. Give decision context

Urgency copy should still include price, terms, alternatives, and consequences.

### 4. Separate possession value from use value

Scarcity can affect collectible or status value; it does not make ordinary utility better. Cialdini makes this defense point in the scarcity chapter.

## Guidelines

- Use exact numbers when they are reliable; use cautious wording when they are not.
- For high-stakes purchases, add cooling-off or follow-up options instead of now-or-never framing.
- If competition is real, state it plainly without staging rival demand.

## Ethical Boundaries

- Use only honest evidence. Do not invent popularity, scarcity, credentials, endorsements, or social connection.
- Separate helping a good decision from pushing a shortcut response. If the cue is counterfeit, treat it as a red flag.
- When rewriting, preserve user agency: add context, alternatives, and enough time to decide when stakes are meaningful.

## Exceptions

- **Parody, critique, or analysis**: Describe manipulative tactics when the purpose is detection, education, or safer rewriting.
- **Real constraints**: Scarcity, authority, popularity, or reciprocity cues are acceptable when accurate, scoped, and relevant.
- **Tiny low-stakes choices**: Lighter disclosure may be fine, but never fabricate evidence.
