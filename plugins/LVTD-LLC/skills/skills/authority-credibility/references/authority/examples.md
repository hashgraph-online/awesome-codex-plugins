# Authority Examples

Examples are paraphrased applications of the principle, not book excerpts.

## Bad and Better Examples

### Advisor board

Bad: Put advisor logos beside a product claim they did not review. Better: list advisors with roles and areas of input.

### Health page

Bad: 'Doctor recommended' with no context. Better: identify who reviewed the content, their credential, date, and scope.

### Security badge

Bad: Display a generic shield icon as if certified. Better: name the actual audit, standard, or control status.

## Rewrite Pattern

1. Identify the active cue.
2. Remove unsupported claims or coercive framing.
3. Add the real evidence, constraint, or user benefit.
4. Make the next step clear but optional.

## Mini Review Example

**Input**: A message uses pressure but does not show evidence.

**Output**:
- Principle: Authority
- Risk: unsupported cue may create a shortcut response.
- Rewrite: state the real reason, proof, or constraint; remove fake pressure.
