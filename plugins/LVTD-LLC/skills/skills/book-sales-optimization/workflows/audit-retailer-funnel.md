# Audit Retailer Funnel Workflow

Use this workflow to audit a book page on Amazon or another retailer.

## When To Use

- A useful book is getting traffic but under-converting.
- The cover, title, description, reviews, or formats may be limiting sales.
- The author wants post-launch optimization ideas.

## Prerequisites

- Book title, target reader, and promise.
- Retailer URL or page copy/screenshots.
- Current platform rules for any specific operational recommendation.

**Reference**: `references/core/rules.md`

---

## Workflow Steps

### Step 1: Verify Current Rules

**Goal**: Avoid stale platform advice.

- [ ] Check official or primary sources for platform policies relevant to the task.
- [ ] Note unknowns and avoid precise claims where rules were not verified.
- [ ] Separate general strategy from platform-specific implementation.

---

### Step 2: Audit Click-Through

**Goal**: Identify why shoppers would or would not click.

- [ ] Review cover at thumbnail size.
- [ ] Check whether title/subtitle reveal reader and promise.
- [ ] Check review count, rating, and social proof.
- [ ] Compare against nearby alternatives if available.

---

### Step 3: Audit Conversion

**Goal**: Identify why visitors would or would not buy.

- [ ] Review description for reader outcomes.
- [ ] Check skimmability.
- [ ] Check sample or look-inside value.
- [ ] Check author profile, endorsements, product images, and retailer fields.
- [ ] Check call to action or purchase clarity for direct-sales pages.

---

### Step 4: Recommend Fixes

**Goal**: Order changes by leverage and control.

- [ ] Start with cover/title/subtitle if click-through is weak.
- [ ] Start with description/proof/sample if conversion is weak.
- [ ] Recommend authentic review plan only within current policies.
- [ ] Identify reader-language inputs needed from beta feedback or reviews.

---

### Step 5: Evaluate Expansion

**Goal**: Decide whether extra formats or channels are worth it.

- [ ] List possible boosts.
- [ ] Score expected reach and profit.
- [ ] Score time, money, maintenance, and stress.
- [ ] Recommend defer, test, or implement.

## Exit Criteria

Task is complete when:

- [ ] Main funnel bottleneck is named.
- [ ] Fixes are ordered by expected impact.
- [ ] Platform-specific claims are verified or clearly marked as needing verification.
