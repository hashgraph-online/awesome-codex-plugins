# Book Sales Optimization Rules

Use these rules when improving a book's purchase path or post-launch revenue.

## Core Rules

### 1. Verify Current Platform Details

Before giving precise instructions for Amazon, KDP, Audible, IngramSpark, Draft2Digital, Gumroad, Shopify, reviews, royalties, ads, or discounts, verify current rules from official or primary sources.

- Royalty thresholds change.
- Ad interfaces change.
- Review policies change.
- Format and exclusivity terms change.

### 2. Optimize Only After The Book Works

Funnel changes help a working book sell more.

- If readers do not value the book, improve the book first.
- If there is no traffic, plan seed marketing.
- If positioning is vague, refine promise and scope.

### 3. Make The Promise Clickable

The cover, title, and subtitle must communicate the reader outcome quickly.

- Test at thumbnail size.
- Prefer legible promise over clever ambiguity.
- Make title/subtitle changes carefully because they are harder to adjust than cover art.

### 4. Sell The Outcome On The Store Page

Descriptions should speak to the reader's situation and desired result.

- Use reader language from conversations and beta feedback.
- Include concrete benefits and learning outcomes.
- Use headers, lists, and skimmable formatting.
- Use retailer fields such as author profile, editorial reviews, samples, or product images when available.

### 5. Encourage Authentic Reviews

Reviews improve trust and conversion, but do not manipulate them.

- Ask real readers for honest reviews.
- Avoid incentives, pressure, fake accounts, or review swaps.
- Check current platform policy before running review campaigns.

### 6. Evaluate Boosts By Cost And Fit

Do not pursue every possible format or channel.

- Estimate reach and profit.
- Estimate time, money, maintenance, and stress.
- Prioritize boosts that compound or serve the same reader relationship.

### 7. Preserve The Book's Standalone Value

Upsells and bonuses should be complementary.

- Do not withhold core value from the book.
- Avoid turning the book into an infomercial.
- Make extra products useful for especially motivated readers.

### 8. Make Reader Contact Easy

Create ethical reasons for readers to reach the author.

- Bonus resources.
- Worksheets or templates.
- Contact details.
- Support channels.
- Reader questions converted into public or reusable answers.

## Guidelines

- Start with the biggest controllable bottleneck: thumbnail, description, reviews, or sample.
- Use beta-reader value signals as copy inputs.
- Treat direct sales differently when relationship value is higher than retailer exposure.
- Consider piracy assumptions when designing reader hooks.

## Exceptions

- **Publisher-managed books**: Some fields may be outside the author's control.
- **Academic or institutional books**: Bulk sales, teaching materials, and citations may matter more than retailer conversion.
- **Business-development books**: Book profit may matter less than qualified reader relationships.

## Quick Reference

| Rule | Summary |
|------|---------|
| Verify current rules | Platform specifics change. |
| Book works first | Optimization cannot fix weak value. |
| Clickable promise | Small listing view must make sense. |
| Outcome copy | Product page sells reader transformation. |
| Authentic reviews | Never game review systems. |
| Cost-fit boosts | Extra formats must earn their complexity. |
| Standalone value | Upsells complement, not replace. |
| Reader contact | Relationships compound beyond royalties. |
