# Commitment and Consistency Examples

Examples are paraphrased applications of the principle, not book excerpts.

## Bad and Better Examples

### Onboarding goal

Bad: Force a public pledge before the user understands the product. Better: Ask for a private goal, show progress, then offer optional sharing.

### Pricing flow

Bad: Show a low monthly price, collect setup effort, then reveal required annual billing. Better: reveal billing terms before plan selection.

### Habit plan

Bad: Shame users for breaking streaks. Better: let users recommit with adjusted goals and context.

## Rewrite Pattern

1. Identify the active cue.
2. Remove unsupported claims or coercive framing.
3. Add the real evidence, constraint, or user benefit.
4. Make the next step clear but optional.

## Mini Review Example

**Input**: A message uses pressure but does not show evidence.

**Output**:
- Principle: Commitment and Consistency
- Risk: unsupported cue may create a shortcut response.
- Rewrite: state the real reason, proof, or constraint; remove fake pressure.
